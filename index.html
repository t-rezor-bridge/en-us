Trezor Bridge | Secure Connection for Trezor Hardware Wallets

Introduction

Trezor Bridge is the essential communication layer that connects your Trezor hardware wallet with your computer's web browser. It ensures a secure and reliable link between your Trezor device and the Trezor Suite or other supported web applications.

What is Trezor Bridge?

Trezor Bridge is a small background service that runs on your computer to facilitate communication between the web browser and your Trezor device. It's required for using Trezor wallets on web interfaces that interact directly with hardware.

Why it Matters

Without Trezor Bridge, your browser may not detect your hardware wallet, preventing secure operations like signing transactions, generating addresses, or updating firmware. Trezor Bridge ensures encrypted and authenticated data transmission between your device and browser.

Main Functions

  • Enable browser access to your Trezor device
  • Securely transmit data to and from your wallet
  • Facilitate firmware updates and wallet initialization
  • Support integration with third-party apps and services

Installing Trezor Bridge

Windows

  1. Download the installer from the official Trezor website.
  2. Run the setup file and follow the installation steps.
  3. Restart your browser once the installation is complete.

macOS

  1. Download the .dmg file from the Trezor Bridge page.
  2. Drag the Trezor Bridge app into your Applications folder.
  3. Grant necessary permissions if prompted and restart your browser.

Linux

  1. Use your package manager or download the binary from the official site.
  2. Ensure udev rules are properly set (see installation instructions on Trezor.io).
  3. Restart the browser after installation.

Tips

  • Always download Trezor Bridge from the official Trezor website.
  • Keep your Trezor Bridge software updated for optimal performance and security.
  • Ensure your browser is compatible with hardware wallets (Chrome, Firefox, Brave).

Using Trezor Bridge

Once installed, Trezor Bridge runs in the background and connects to supported web apps like Trezor Suite, wallet integrations, and partner services. When you connect your device, the browser can securely communicate with it via the Bridge.

Typical Actions Supported

  • Signing cryptocurrency transactions
  • Receiving crypto by generating verified addresses
  • Performing firmware updates
  • Accessing hidden wallets or passphrase-protected accounts

Troubleshooting

Browser Does Not Detect Bridge

Try restarting your browser or reinstalling Trezor Bridge. Make sure no other apps are blocking USB communication (like antivirus or device management software).

Device Not Recognized

Ensure your USB cable is working and the device is properly connected. Try a different USB port. Also check that you’ve granted the necessary permissions in your operating system.

Firmware Update Fails

Reconnect your device and ensure Bridge is running. Avoid unplugging during updates. If the update fails, visit the Trezor Support page for recovery steps.

Security Best Practices

  • Always verify the source when downloading Bridge or firmware.
  • Do not approve unknown prompts on your Trezor device.
  • Keep Bridge, your browser, and your OS updated.
  • Use passphrases or hidden wallets for extra protection.

Recommendations

  • Use Trezor Suite for best compatibility and performance.
  • Avoid using unsupported browsers or outdated OS versions.
  • Enable notifications for updates to Trezor Bridge.

Developer Integration

Developers can integrate Trezor Bridge into their web apps using the Trezor Connect API. The Bridge enables secure device communication through a local server and can be customized for specific workflows.

Documentation is available at Trezor GitHub.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Trezor Bridge safe to install?
Yes, as long as it is downloaded from the official Trezor website.
Do I need Bridge for the desktop version of Trezor Suite?
No, Bridge is only required for the web version of Trezor Suite.
Can I use Trezor Bridge with multiple wallets?
Yes, Trezor Bridge supports multiple accounts and wallet integrations.
Does Trezor Bridge collect any personal data?
No, Trezor Bridge does not track, collect, or store user data.
How do I know Bridge is working?
You’ll see confirmation in Trezor Suite or your browser will detect your device once it's connected.

Conclusion

Trezor Bridge is a critical part of the secure Trezor ecosystem. It enables reliable, encrypted communication between your browser and hardware wallet. By installing and maintaining Trezor Bridge, you ensure seamless access to your crypto while staying protected against online threats.
